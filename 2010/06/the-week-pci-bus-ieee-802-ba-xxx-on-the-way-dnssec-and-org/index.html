The Week: - PCI bus, IEEE 802.ba, .xxx on the way, DNSSEC and .ORG

Created2010-06-25Updated2020-08-09

Intel is moving on, time to say good bye to PCI Local Bus not because it doesn’t work but instead technology is moving faster than the 1993 Bus can support. IEEE ratifies 802.3ba aka 40Gb/s and 100Gb/s ethernet, a sing of increases in throughput to come. .XXX may become a reality, ICANN will make the announcement on its decision on Friday. Controversial cybersecurity bill approved by US senate panel, will the government be granted a kill switch?. Domain Name System Security Extensions or DNSSEC is now responsible for securing the .ORG TLD. Hopefully, domain registrars will follow.
